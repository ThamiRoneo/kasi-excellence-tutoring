Our Mission

At Kasi Excellence Tutoring, we believe that every learner in every township deserves access to the same quality education as learners in well-resourced schools. Our mission is simple but powerful: to empower township learners to pursue STEM careers by providing world-class, affordable, and culturally relevant tutoring.

We are tutors from your community. We understand the challenges — the crowded classrooms, the limited resources, the pressure of matric — and we are here to bridge the gap with knowledge, dedication, and genuine care.

How It All Began

Our Story

Kasi Excellence Tutoring was born out of frustration and hope. Our founders, both products of the township school system, watched year after year as brilliant young minds in their community struggled — not because they lacked ability, but because they lacked access. Access to patient, personalised teaching. Access to someone who understood both the CAPS curriculum and the realities of township life.


The name "Kasi Excellence" was chosen deliberately. Kasi — the South African word for township — is not a place of limitation. It is a place of resilience, creativity, and untapped potential. We wanted to reclaim that word and attach excellence to it, because excellence belongs here just as much as it belongs anywhere else.


We started with a handful of Grade 10 students around a kitchen table. The sessions were informal, the notes were handwritten, and the results were extraordinary. Word spread quickly through the community.

Within months, our small tutoring group had grown into a structured programme covering Mathematics, Physical Sciences, and Accounting — the three subjects that most determine whether a learner can access tertiary education in South Africa.


Today, Kasi Excellence Tutoring continues to grow, driven by one unwavering belief: that the brightest doctors, engineers, accountants, and scientists of tomorrow are sitting in township classrooms right now. They just need someone to unlock the door.


Our Approach: Local-first teaching. Relatable analogies drawn from everyday township life. Mother tongue support where needed. And an unflinching focus on the specific demands of South African matric exams.
